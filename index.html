Recipe #1

  • Time: 1 hour and 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4-5 people
Ingredients Quantity
boneless chicken, cut into cubes 800 grams
plain yogurt 1 cup
lemon juice 3 tablespoons
ginger paste 2 tablespoons
garlic paste 2 tablespoons
red chili powder 2 teaspoons
turmeric powder 1 teaspoons
Salt to taste
butter 6 tablespoons
vegetable oil 1 tablespoon
finely chopped onions 2 large
garam masala 2 teaspoons
cumin powder 2 teaspoons
coriander powdert 2 teaspoons
tomato puree 2 cups
heavy cream 1 cup
dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) 2 tablespoons
Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, ginger paste, garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or refrigerate overnight for better flavor.
  2. Heat 4 tablespoons of butter and the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces, discarding any excess marinade, and cook until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter and chopped onions. Saut√© the onions until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add garam masala, cumin powder, and coriander powder to the pan and cook for a minute to release the flavors.
  5. Add tomato puree to the pan and cook for a few minutes until the oil separates from the sauce and the tomatoes are cooked.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, add the heavy cream, and stir well. Cook for a couple of minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. Return the cooked chicken to the pan and mix it well with the sauce. Simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  8. Crush the dried fenugreek leaves between your palms and add them to the pan. Stir well.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little water or more cream to achieve the desired consistency.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Recipe #2

  • Time: 2 hours
  • Serves: 4-5 people
Ingredients Quantity
basmati rice 500 grams
chicken, mutton, or vegetables (your choice) 500 grams
thinly sliced onions 2 large
chopped 4 tomatoes
ginger-garlic paste 2 tablespoons
plain yogurt 1 cup
biryani masala powder 2 teaspoons
turmeric powder 1 teaspoon
red chili powder 1 teaspoon
cumin seeds 1 teaspoon
green cardamom pods 4-5
cloves 4-5
cinnamon stick 2-inch
ghee or vegetable oil 4 tablespoons
fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
Fresh mint leaves for garnish
Salt to taste
Saffron strands (optional) soaked in milk for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee or vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and set aside for garnish.
  3. To the remaining onions in the pot, add cumin seeds, green cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Stir for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices.
  6. Add the chicken, mutton, or vegetables to the pot. Stir and cook until they are partially cooked.
  7. In a small bowl, mix yogurt, biryani masala powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Add this mixture to the pot and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined
  8. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Gently mix the rice with the meat and spices.
  9. Add enough water to the pot to cook the rice. The ratio of rice to water is typically 1:1.5, but it may vary, so follow the instructions on your rice packet for the correct water amount.
  10. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
  11. Once the biryani is cooked, let it sit covered for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  12. Garnish the biryani with the reserved fried onions, fresh cilantro leaves, mint leaves, and saffron soaked in milk (optional).
